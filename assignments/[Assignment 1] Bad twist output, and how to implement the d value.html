    Input to sine and cosine functions are  in radian.     You convert theta  degree into radian by  multiplying it by a factor of Math.PI/180.

    SinT = Math.sin( theta * Math.PI/180 )
    cosT = Math.cos( theta * Math.PI/180 )

    Deepak Sinha· 10 days ago 
    Tessellation is what decides how many vertex you will end up with, so of course you have to  Tessellate first.    Your twisting is way too complicated!     It should be max 5 lines of code.
    I recommend doing the twisting/rotation inside the  Vertex Shader.  For that you will need to send theta from your application program to the vertex shader using a uniform variable.

    Calculating the rotation is  simple, just use the roatation formula,   but before that calculate the distance d which is multiplied by  theta before using the Sine and Cosine functions.  d is just sqrt(x^2+y^2).

    Take a look at Professor Angel's  EXAMPLES/rotatingSquare2.html  vertext shader,    very little modification is needed. Multiply theta by d and rearrange 'c' and 's' in   equations.
